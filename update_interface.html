How to Update Working Locations

Note: Working locations must be updated directly in Google Calendar. This ensures proper synchronization and permissions.

1 Open Google Calendar

Go to calendar.google.com and sign in with your account.

2 Enable Working Location

Click on the settings gear (⚙️) in the top right corner and select "Settings".

In the left sidebar, click on "General" and find the "Working location" section.

Toggle on "Enable working location".

3 Set Your Daily Location

There are two ways to set your working location:

Option A: From Calendar View

  1. Navigate to the day you want to set
  2. Look for the working location indicator below the date
  3. Click on it and select your location

Option B: Recurring Schedule

  1. In Settings → Working location
  2. Set up a recurring schedule (e.g., "Office on Mon-Wed, Home on Thu-Fri")
  3. Google Calendar will automatically apply this pattern

4 Custom Locations

You can also set custom locations like:

  • "Client Site"
  • "Conference"
  • "Co-working Space"
  • Or any specific location name
Important: After updating your working locations in Google Calendar, the changes will appear in the team dashboard after the next automatic update (or when an admin runs the update script).

5 Sharing Your Calendar

If you're a new team member, make sure your calendar is shared with the admin:

  1. In Google Calendar, find your calendar in the left sidebar
  2. Click the 3 dots menu → "Settings and sharing"
  3. Under "Share with specific people", add the admin's email
  4. Grant "See all event details" permission
